Dismissal for Delay



A criminal case will get dismissed if it takes too long to bring to trial.  The exact time limit varies from case to case but it is usually 18 months for less serious offences and 30 months for more serious offences.


How it Works


To get a case dismissed for delay you have to show that the delay was caused by the police and not by you.   The hardest part of the prosecutor's job before a trial is making sure you have a copy of all the evidence available in the case   When they don't, you can create a delay that counts against them by refusing to book a trial date until the evidence is provided.


Some quick examples of evidence to ask for are videos from the police car and the police station.   Make sure you have a list of all the officers involved in your case, and make sure you have notes for all the police officers.   There are also charge specific things police must provide.   For example D.U.I charges have a certificate from the breath tech, as well as the data from the breath testing device.


Although it seems unlikely to do this for 18 months, remember that it sometimes takes a year or even longer to get a trial date.   So a few months tacked on could easily get a case dismissed
