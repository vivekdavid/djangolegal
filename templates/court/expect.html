Criminal Court



Your first time in court will be in something called set date court.     Set date court serves the following functions:

  • Making sure the prosecutor has given you a copy all the evidence that will be used against you
  • Seeing if your case can be resolved without a trial
  • Setting a date for trial.

You will have to go to court about once a month until these administrative areas are taken care of and your case is resolved or a trial date is set.

Usually, your first appearence will be nothing more than seeing if the prosecutor has the evidence ready to give you.   As your third and fourth appeareances come up, the court will want to know if you have hired a lawyer.   Around this time you will be expected to either set a date for trial or make a deal with the prosecutor.

You will not be allowed to argue your case at set-date court. This is reserved for trial.
