Impaired Driving


How to start driving in 3 months after a DUI


Impaired driving is punishable with a 1 year driving suspension for a first offence.   However, is is possible to negotiate with the prosecutor and get back on the road in as little as 3 months.

To be eligible for the shortened suspension you have to offer to get an ignition interlock device temporally installed in your car.   An ignition interlock is a machine that won't let yout start your car until you pass a breath alcohol test.   The device must stay on your car for between 6 to 9 months.   It costs about $ 200 for installation, $ 150 a month for monitoring and $ 70 to get it removed.

How much your suspension can be shortened with a device is determined by the specific circumstances of your case.   To be eligible for a 3 month suspension, you must plead guilty within 90 days of the offence date.
